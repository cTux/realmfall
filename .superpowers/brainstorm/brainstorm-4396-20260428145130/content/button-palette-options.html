Recommended calm, clear lift

Slightly lighter than the title bar everywhere, with medium slate borders. Keeps the UI disciplined while making click targets read as separate controls.

  • Lowest churn relative to the current palette.
  • Applies well to buttons, tabs, dock entries, and title actions.
  • Safer if you want the windows to stay dominant over the controls.

High contrast brighter controls

Pushes every button noticeably lighter, with clearer borders and more immediate separation from the title bar and body panels.

  • Best if you want controls to pop instantly.
  • Very readable for tabs and dock buttons.
  • Risk: can make busy windows feel louder than the content.

Textured subtle gradient treatment

Keeps the same brightness rule, but uses a more material-like button surface with restrained borders to feel a bit more crafted.

  • Most “designed” option without changing structure.
  • Good fit if you want buttons to feel less flat.
  • Risk: gradients across every surface can get inconsistent fast.
